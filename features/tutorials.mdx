The **Tutoriales** section provides general-purpose installation guides for GPS tracking professionals. Unlike cut point records — which are specific to a single vehicle model and year range — tutorials cover techniques and concepts that apply across many vehicles.

Access tutorials by tapping **Tutoriales** in the main navigation.

<Note>
Tutorials are general guides covering techniques and component identification. For vehicle-specific wiring data (cable colors, cut locations, relay configurations), use the [Cut points](/features/cut-points) section.
</Note>

## What tutorials cover

Tutorials in GPSpedia focus on three main areas:

<Columns cols={3}>
  <Card title="Disassembly techniques" icon="screwdriver-wrench">
    How to safely remove interior trim panels, dashboards, door cards, and center consoles to access wiring harnesses without damaging the vehicle.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cable identification" icon="cable-car">
    How to identify specific wire types, locate the correct harness connectors, and use a multimeter to verify circuits before cutting.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Interior panel removal" icon="layer-group">
    Step-by-step procedures for removing specific panel types — including clip locations, hidden fasteners, and reassembly notes.
  </Card>
</Columns>

## Tutorial structure

Each tutorial record contains the following fields:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Tema (topic)">
    The title of the tutorial — for example, "Cómo identificar el cable de la bomba de combustible" or "Desmontaje de tablero en vehículos japoneses".
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Imagen (cover image)">
    A representative photo or diagram for the tutorial topic. Tap to open in the lightbox.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cómo identificarlo (how to identify)">
    A description of how to recognize the component or situation covered by the tutorial — for example, what a fuel pump relay looks like or how to distinguish ignition wires from accessory wires.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Dónde encontrarlo (where to find it)">
    Guidance on the typical physical location of the component across common vehicle types — for example, under the dashboard on the driver's side, behind the glove box, or in the engine bay fuse block.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Detalles (details)">
    The main body of the tutorial. Contains step-by-step instructions, safety notes, and additional context for the technique being covered.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Video (YouTube guide)">
    An embedded YouTube video demonstrating the technique. The video plays inline within the tutorial view and stops automatically when you close the tutorial or navigate away.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Using tutorials effectively

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Tutoriales section">
    Tap **Tutoriales** in the main navigation to see all available tutorial cards.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a tutorial">
    Browse the topic cards and tap the one relevant to your current task — for example, a panel removal guide before you need to access a dashboard harness.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the identification and location guidance">
    Review the **Cómo identificarlo** and **Dónde encontrarlo** sections first to orient yourself before starting physical work on the vehicle.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow the detailed steps">
    Work through the **Detalles** section. If a video is available, watch it before or alongside the written instructions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to the cut point record">
    Once you have identified the correct harness location or completed the disassembly, navigate back to the specific vehicle's cut point record to confirm the exact wire color and relay configuration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Watch the video guide at reduced playback speed (0.75×) when learning a new disassembly technique. This makes it easier to follow connector clip positions and tool angles before working on an actual vehicle.
</Tip>
